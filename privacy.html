Section 03

How We Collect Your Data

We collect personal data through the following channels:

  • Direct Interactions: When you fill in our contact form, call us, send a WhatsApp message, email us, or visit our office in person.
  • Service Agreements: When you sign a property management contract, tenancy agreement, construction contract, or any other service document with us.
  • Tenant Applications: When prospective tenants submit rental applications, references, or supporting documents for screening purposes.
  • Website Usage: When you browse our website, cookies and analytics tools may collect technical data about your visit automatically.
  • Third Parties: From referral agents, previous landlords, employers (for references), and credit reference agencies where applicable.
  • Public Sources: Publicly available information such as land registry records or business registration databases where relevant to our services.
Section 04

How We Use Your Data

We use your personal data only for legitimate purposes directly related to the services we provide. Specifically, we use your data to:

  • Respond to your enquiries, service requests, and communications promptly.
  • Screen and verify prospective tenants on behalf of property owners.
  • Prepare, execute, and manage tenancy agreements, management contracts, and construction contracts.
  • Collect, process, and disburse rental income and other property-related payments.
  • Produce financial statements, rent rolls, and management reports for property owners.
  • Coordinate maintenance works, inspections, and contractor visits.
  • Comply with our legal obligations under Ghanaian law, including tax and anti-money laundering requirements.
  • Send service updates, invoices, and relevant property information to clients and tenants.
  • Send marketing communications about our services where you have given consent (you may opt out at any time).
  • Improve our website, services, and internal processes using anonymised analytics data.
We will never: sell, rent, or trade your personal information to any third party for their own marketing purposes.
Section 05

Legal Basis for Processing

We process your personal data on the following legal grounds in accordance with applicable Ghanaian data protection law and the Data Protection Act, 2012 (Act 843):

  • Contract Performance: Processing is necessary to perform a contract with you (e.g. managing your property, tenancy administration, construction services).
  • Legal Obligation: Processing is necessary to comply with a legal obligation (e.g. identity verification, tax reporting, anti-money laundering).
  • Legitimate Interests: Processing is necessary for our legitimate business interests (e.g. responding to enquiries, improving our services, preventing fraud).
  • Consent: Where you have given us explicit consent to process your data for a specific purpose, such as receiving marketing communications. You may withdraw consent at any time.
Section 06

Sharing Your Personal Data

We may share your personal data with carefully selected third parties only where necessary and on a need-to-know basis:

  • Property Owners / Landlords: Relevant tenant information is shared with property owners as part of our management services.
  • Tenants: Property owner contact details may be shared with tenants where necessary for urgent or legal matters.
  • Maintenance Contractors: Contact and access details shared with approved contractors to facilitate repairs and maintenance.
  • Legal & Professional Advisors: Solicitors, accountants, or other advisors engaged to support our services or legal obligations.
  • Financial Institutions: Banks or payment processors involved in processing rent collection or service payments.
  • Government & Regulatory Authorities: Where required by law, court order, or regulatory requirement (e.g. Ghana Revenue Authority, Ghana Police Service).
  • Technology Service Providers: Software and IT service providers who support our operations, bound by confidentiality agreements.
International Transfers: We do not routinely transfer personal data outside Ghana. Where any transfer is necessary, we will ensure appropriate safeguards are in place to protect your data.

Section 08

Data Security

Uneek takes data security seriously and has implemented appropriate technical and organisational measures to protect your personal data against unauthorised access, accidental loss, destruction, or disclosure.

  • Physical access to office premises and paper records is restricted to authorised staff only.
  • Electronic records are stored in password-protected systems with access controls.
  • Sensitive documents are stored securely and disposed of through secure shredding.
  • Staff handling personal data are trained on data protection responsibilities.
  • Third-party service providers are required to maintain appropriate security standards.
While we take all reasonable steps to protect your data, no method of transmission over the internet or electronic storage is 100% secure. If you believe your data has been compromised, please contact us immediately.
Section 09

Cookies & Website Tracking

Our website uses cookies — small text files placed on your device — to improve your browsing experience and help us understand how visitors use our site. The cookies we use fall into the following categories:

  • Essential
    Strictly Necessary Cookies: Required for the website to function correctly. These cannot be disabled. They include session management and security cookies.
  • Analytics
    Analytics Cookies: Help us understand how visitors interact with our website (e.g. Google Analytics). All data collected is anonymised and aggregated.
  • Marketing
    Marketing / Preference Cookies: Used to remember your preferences and deliver content relevant to your interests. Only set with your consent.

You can control or disable non-essential cookies through your browser settings at any time. Please note that disabling cookies may affect the functionality of certain parts of our website.

Google Maps: Our website embeds Google Maps iframes, which may set cookies from Google. Please refer to Google's Privacy Policy at policies.google.com/privacy for further information.
Section 10

Your Rights

Under the Data Protection Act, 2012 (Act 843) of Ghana, you have the following rights with respect to your personal data held by Uneek:

  • Right of Access: You may request a copy of the personal data we hold about you, free of charge in most circumstances.
  • Right to Rectification: You may ask us to correct any inaccurate or incomplete personal data we hold about you.
  • Right to Erasure: You may request deletion of your personal data where there is no legitimate reason for us to continue holding it.
  • Right to Restriction: You may ask us to restrict the processing of your data in certain circumstances, for example while a complaint is being investigated.
  • Right to Data Portability: You may request your data in a structured, commonly used, machine-readable format for transfer to another organisation.
  • Right to Object: You may object to the processing of your personal data where we rely on legitimate interests as our legal basis.
  • Right to Withdraw Consent: Where processing is based on your consent, you may withdraw it at any time without affecting the lawfulness of prior processing.

To exercise any of these rights, please contact us using the details in Section 14. We will respond to your request within 30 days. We may need to verify your identity before processing your request.

You also have the right to lodge a complaint with the Data Protection Commission of Ghana if you believe your data protection rights have been violated.

Section 11

Children's Privacy

Our services are not directed at children under the age of 18. We do not knowingly collect personal data from minors. If you are a parent or guardian and believe your child has provided us with personal information without your knowledge, please contact us immediately and we will promptly delete the relevant data.

Where children are listed as occupants of a managed property in a tenancy agreement, only the minimum necessary information (such as number and ages of occupants) will be recorded, in accordance with our legal obligations as a property manager.

Section 12

Third-Party Links

Our website may contain links to third-party websites, social media platforms, or embedded content (such as Google Maps or Facebook). These third-party sites have their own privacy policies which we have no control over.

  • We are not responsible for the privacy practices of any third-party website or platform.
  • Clicking on a third-party link will direct you away from our site, and their privacy policy will apply to your visit.
  • We recommend reviewing the privacy policy of any third-party site before providing your personal information.

Our social media pages on platforms such as Facebook, Instagram, LinkedIn, and WhatsApp are subject to those platforms' own privacy policies and terms of service.
